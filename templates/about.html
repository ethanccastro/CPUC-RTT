    • About RTT Network

    RTT Network manually analyzes, aggregate, and visualize data from the California Public Utilities Comission (CPUC) to provide information regarding the four major carriers (Sprint, Verizon, T-Mobile, AT&T) in the United States.

    Our data is: replicable, reliable, and trusted. We encourage our audience to use the information to help make guided decisions on their choice of service, or inspire to create their own statistics and creativity to further enhance the graphs that are displayed.

    Methods

    Part of our work consists of monitoring the years from 2012-2020 from all of the major carriers in the west and east coasts of the United States. We sourced our data from the CPUC website and carefully crafted a large datasheet that contains all the information downloaded from the website. There were little to no modifications on the actual data, as we hoped that other people would be able to find themselves creating their own visualizations.

    The data was divided into several dataframes that originated from all the combined CSV files. We looked at the top 10%, 20%... 50% of the best values for each of the RTT and TCP metrics to find trends and correlations to grab insight about the carriers.

    Definitions

    Round-trip time (RTT) is the duration in ms for a network request to go from a starting point and back again. Also known as ping, this metric can inform phone users how fast they are able to communicate and navigate through the internet, applications, or anything that uses their service. The speed is determined by how fast in ms the data is received and sent back. The lower in ms, the faster the speed gets.

    Transmission Control Protocol (TCP) is one of the main protocols of the Internet protocol suite. It enables applications and devices to transmit data by dividing messages into packets which then can be forwarded through switches and routers to the destination. Because these applications and devices are packing and unpacking the data, TCP is divided into upload and download speed. Upload speed is when a device is sending a message, and download speed is when a device is receiving a message. It is important to have both of these to be as high as it can be.

    Difference TCP needs to determine RTT for good performance. For each connection, TCP will continiously estimate the RTT and adjust how the messages are sent for each packet that is read. The most important distinction regarded the ability to question whether RTT or TCP can determine the accuracy in which data is sent is RTT's role in assisting TCP's computed time to send out packets. RTT Network recommends seeing the correlation between RTT and TCP by comparing several graphs together. One trend to look out for is how the values increase or decrease. Intuitively, if they were to correlate, RTT would decrease and TCP would increase.

    California Public Utilities Comission (CPUC) is a department of California utilizing CalSpeed project teams to use smartphones and tablets at 2,000 points to measure mobile broadbant service availability. Their data sheets contain summarized data that is the average of the 160 individual tests for each metric (RTT and TCP) measured at each point tested. Their data is available for public use and modification.
