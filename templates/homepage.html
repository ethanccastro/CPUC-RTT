Introduction

As shown by the graphic above, RTT patterns appear to trend downwards, implying that ping times are continuing to decrease from years 2012-2020. The highest average among all carriers peaked at about 51ms in 2013, while the lowest was 45ms in 2020. As 5G becomes more common at more data points are set up around California, we can predict that the times would continue to decrease.



By 2020, Verizon topped out as the best provider with the lowest RTT at around 45ms. T-Mobile and Sprint appears to be trending upwards, meanwhile AT&T and Verizon trends downwards. There was some slight discrepancies from years 2014-2016 as most carriers faced lower RTT speeds but it smoothes out onwards.



Some of the values of the RTT time over the years for each provider. Clearly, Verizon has maintained the best speeds. At around 47 ms, Verizon can claim that they had the best speeds over the years. Meanwhile, T-Mobile has maintained one of the worst speeds and Sprint had logged the worst RTT ms at around 53ms.

Visualizations

Select percentile

Verizon clocked in the best RTT average for the top 10% with At&t as a close second. However, for the top 20% and 50%, At&t overtakes Verizon as the leading performer with Firstnet, Sprint, and T-Moblie trailing, respectively. Upon calculating the average RTT for each provider, we discovered that the data entered for 2012 from Sprint did not enter the top 50 percentile due to low performance. Another thing that stood out to us was that FirstNet was third in terms of top 20% and 50% but placed last in the top 10 percentile. This can attributed to the lack of records from this provider, considering that the CPUC only began recording their performance in 2020.

Select percentile

The data recorded for the east server shows that Verizon was the clear dominator placing first in each percentile. At&t placed second across the board, with the remaining carriers interchanging positions for each category. When comparing the average RTT between the east and west server, there is a clear discrepancy in performance with the east server being considerably slower.

    • About RTT Network

    RTT Network manually analyzes, aggregate, and visualize data from the California Public Utilities Comission (CPUC) to provide information regarding the four major carriers (Sprint, Verizon, T-Mobile, AT&T) in the United States.

    Our data is: replicable, reliable, and trusted. We encourage our audience to use the information to help make guided decisions on their choice of service, or inspire to create their own statistics and creativity to further enhance the graphs that are displayed.

    Methods

    Part of our work consists of monitoring the years from 2012-2020 from all of the major carriers in the west and east coasts of the United States. We sourced our data from the CPUC website and carefully crafted a large datasheet that contains all the information downloaded from the website. There were little to no modifications on the actual data, as we hoped that other people would be able to find themselves creating their own visualizations.

    The data was divided into several dataframes that originated from all the combined CSV files. We looked at the top 10%, 20%... 50% of the best values for each of the RTT and TCP metrics to find trends and correlations to grab insight about the carriers.

    Definitions

    Round-trip time (RTT)

    The duration in ms for a network request to go from a starting point and back again. Also known as ping, this metric can inform phone users how fast they are able to communicate and navigate through the internet, applications, or anything that uses their service. The speed is determined by how fast in ms the data is received and sent back. The lower in ms, the faster the speed gets.

    Transmission Control Protocol (TCP)

    One of the main protocols of the Internet protocol suite. It enables applications and devices to transmit data by dividing messages into packets which then can be forwarded through switches and routers to the destination. Because these applications and devices are packing and unpacking the data, TCP is divided into upload and download speed. Upload speed is when a device is sending a message, and download speed is when a device is receiving a message. It is important to have both of these to be as high as it can be.

    Difference

    TCP needs to determine RTT for good performance. For each connection, TCP will continiously estimate the RTT and adjust how the messages are sent for each packet that is read. The most important distinction regarded the ability to question whether RTT or TCP can determine the accuracy in which data is sent is RTT's role in assisting TCP's computed time to send out packets. RTT Network recommends seeing the correlation between RTT and TCP by comparing several graphs together. One trend to look out for is how the values increase or decrease. Intuitively, if they were to correlate, RTT would decrease and TCP would increase.

    California Public Utilities Comission (CPUC)

    A department of California utilizing CalSpeed project teams to use smartphones and tablets at 2,000 points to measure mobile broadbant service availability. Their data sheets contain summarized data that is the average of the 160 individual tests for each metric (RTT and TCP) measured at each point tested. Their data is available for public use and modification.

    Data

    These are all data sourced from the original CPUC website. All file names correspond to the years that the datapoints were recorded. Atrribute information varies as new datasheets integrated more information, but most of the datasheets contain columns. After combining all the datasheets, one contains all a master dataframe with all the information, along with separate datasheets containing only top values. (Only from west servers)
